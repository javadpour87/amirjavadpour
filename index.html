Research journey

From infrastructure optimization to adaptive cybersecurity systems.

The trajectory evolves from cloud, SDN, and resource optimization toward intelligent cyber defense, programmable network security, and trust-centric protection for complex distributed environments.

Foundation in networks, cloud systems, and optimization

2015–2020

Early work focused on wireless systems, cloud resource allocation, SDN, and optimization-driven infrastructure management—building the systems background that later shaped the security research agenda.

Shift toward intelligent security and cyber-physical trust

2021–2023

This stage expanded into intrusion detection, privacy-aware architectures, cloud-IoT protection, network slicing security, and early moving target defense concepts supported by AI and learning-based methods.

Flagship work on MTD, 5G slicing security, deception, and trust

2024–2026

Recent publications sharpen the focus on adaptive defense strategies for next-generation infrastructures: RL-based slice isolation and mutation, cyber deception surveys, EaaS trust architectures, secure path verification, service mesh hardening, and edge-focused DDoS mitigation.
